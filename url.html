URL

Definition: A URL (Uniform Resource Locator) is the address of a specific resource on the internet, such as a webpage, image, or file.

A URL consists of several parts, including the protocol (e.g., http or https), the domain name, and the path to the specific resource.

URLs are essential for navigating the internet, as they provide a standardized way to locate and access resources online.
